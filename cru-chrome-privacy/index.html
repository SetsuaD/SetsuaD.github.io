CRU Chrome — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-30

CRU Chrome is an internal extension for Computer Repair Unlimited (CRU) staff and authorized scribes. It is distributed unlisted and intended for authorized users only.

What it does with data

  • Proxy credentials: Your CRU proxy username is stored locally in the browser. Your password is kept only for the session by default, and stored locally only if you explicitly check "remember on this device." Credentials are sent only to CRU's own proxy server to satisfy its authentication challenge.
  • mTLS client certificate: The extension does not hold or transmit any private key. Your CRU client certificate lives in your operating system's certificate store (Windows Certificate Store / macOS Keychain / Linux NSS); Chrome presents it directly to CRU's proxy.
  • Entitlements: When present, the extension reads your feature entitlements from CRU-Certified using the certificate already in your OS store. No separate login.

What it does NOT do

  • Does not sell or transfer your data to third parties.
  • Does not use data for any purpose unrelated to providing CRU's proxy and internal tools.
  • Does not read, collect, or transmit the content of the web pages you visit.
  • Does not use data to determine creditworthiness or for lending.

Data retention

Credentials are stored locally on your device only and are removed when you disconnect (session password) or clear the extension's storage. CRU's proxy logs connection metadata (authenticated username, destination host, timestamp) for security and auditing, per CRU's internal policy.

Contact

For questions, contact CRU IT at team@computerrepairunlimited.com.
